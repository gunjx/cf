FAQ

Why did you decide to become a web developer?

I’ve been working for some time in online marketing. My tasks involve writing content and optimizing SEO pages as well as designing landing pages for SEM advertising. What always frustrated me was the fact that I had to ask the web developers of my clients to change the website and landing pages instead of implementing the changes myself. My inability to modify websites according to the marketing requirements often delayed the project or consumed valuable time of core developers. That’s why in order to give my clients a better and well-rounded service experience, I decided to learn web development.

Who are your clients?

My clients are internet startups in their early stages. The founders oftentimes have built websites without marketing needs in mind. I help these startups grow through online customer and user acquisition. I also help my clients with the technical implementation to save their time and effort. Right now, I am expanding into a second client base that is non-technical and wants to have a simple yet good looking website.

Who are your references?

I have worked for dawawas.com, an online photosharing platform with over 100,000 registered users. I helped create and optimize their content landing pages and jumpstarted the website’s non-paid organic traffic to over 1,000 registrations a month. For a small business owner in the real estate business, I have created and implemented google ads as well as the corresponding landing pages. The measures have spurred user growth.

What makes you a good web developer?

First of all, I am a problem solver at heart. At times puzzling problems can become frustrating. But I love the rewarding feeling when I finally unravel the mystery. I think my clients benefit greatly from my endurance and passion for what I do.
